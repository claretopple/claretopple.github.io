Where my passion comes from

When I was young, I spent alot of time with my mother, learning to sew and make clothes. I grew up making alot of my own dresses and helping to make dresses for my sisters. I got my first sewing machine at 8 and the love and passion for sewing exploded from there. My love for food started when I was little and helped my mother cook every weekend. We would bake cakes and sweets for friends and neighbours. Cake making and decorating became a huge passion when I had children and made my eldest's first birthday cake. I decided to turn this passion into a business where I not only got to make some wonderful cakes, but developed my skills and experimented with different flavour combinations. Recently I have been researching Indigenous foods and how to cook with them.

About Me

My name is Clare Topple and I am a Design and Technology pre-service teacher. I am currently completing a Bachelor of Education (Secondary)/Bachelor of Arts (Technology) degree at the Australian Catholic University. I have come to this degree as a mature age student, after many years working in the hospitality industry in training and development, running a cake decorating business, a love for all things textiles and craft and personal family experience with children who have learning difficulties. This, combined with my passion for the food and textiles industries, has led me to this degree and a future in teaching. I am now striving to become a Design and Technology Teacher who supports, inspires and encourages creative and innovative future designers.
